Learning Use Your Drone

Learning to use an drone for fun or for business can be difficult, fun, and and rewarding. The following website will introduce you to the basics of drone hardware, flight basics and continuing education, including the certification needed for commercial use.

Drone Hardware

Drones come in all shapes and sizes. There are racing drones made for speed and maneuvaribility, aerial photography, as well as government and military applications. This is not a discussion of large scale "military strike" drones, but rather, relatively small battery operated types. Most drones consist of these basic parts:

Propellers

Drones typically employ four propellers to elevate and stabilize the aircraft. There are, however, many different designs that incorporate as many as eight or more propellers. Stabilization is achieved by creating an equal downward force from each propeller. Moving forward can be achieved by having slightly less force from the front propellers than the rear, left by more force from the right, etc.

Communications

All drones must have a system in place to communicate with the user. This system could simply be a Bluetooth connection to your smartphone, or an advanced proprietary high speed data transmission system.

Flight Control System

The "Brains" of the drone are in the flight control systems. These systems can vary from basic communications and power systems, to advanced automated flight controls, telemetry systems, and photography controls. The most basic requirements of a FCS is some type of physical input system (remote controller), communications with the user, and pushing those outputs to the propellers.

Flight Basics

Learning to fly an aerial drone can be a very challenging endeavor, depending on the device being used. It is usually recommended to begin learning how to fly using a cheaper, and durable, drone. The benfit of this, besides not destroying a $1,000 device, is that the cheaper drones have less automated flight features, allowing you to learn the controls without much help. Once you have mastered these controlls, the more advanced drones will be simple to fly.

Controls

The most basic drone controls usually employ two joysticks. The left stick controls the altitude and rotation, while the right stick controls directional movement.

Continuing Education

Learning to fly a drone can be as involved and as detailed as you would like it to be. To use a drone in commercial applications, you will need to pass an exam and register your drone for commercial use. The FAA controls this process.
